1. Script Writer

Role Overview:
As a Script Writer, you’ll be the creative force behind our video and stage productions, developing original scripts and adapting concepts into compelling narratives.

Responsibilities:

  • Brainstorm and pitch story ideas with the creative team.
  • Research topics, themes, and settings for authenticity.
  • Write, edit, and revise scripts for short films, interviews, and promotional videos.
  • Collaborate with directors and producers to align scripts with production goals.
  • Incorporate feedback from team reviews and script readings.
  • Develop character profiles and dialogue.
  • Format scripts according to industry standards.

Qualifications:

  • Strong creative writing and storytelling skills.
  • Ability to research and synthesize information.
  • Familiarity with script
  • Excellent grammar and attention to detail.
  • Ability to work under deadlines and accept constructive criticism.
  • Portfolio or writing samples (preferred).

Benefits:

  • Mentorship from experienced writers and directors.
  • Portfolio pieces for future opportunities.
  • Exposure to the full production process.
  • Opportunities to attend script readings and workshops.

2. Videographer

Role Overview:
As a Videographer, you’ll capture high-quality video content, working closely with the production team to bring creative visions to life.

Responsibilities:

  • Set up and operate cameras, tripods, and other equipment.
  • Plan and execute shot lists with directors.
  • Adjust lighting and sound for optimal video quality.
  • Record interviews, events, and behind-the-scenes footage.
  • Manage and organize raw video files.
  • Ensure all equipment is maintained and stored properly.
  • Assist with location scouting and logistics.

Qualifications:

  • Basic to intermediate knowledge of camera operation (DSLR, mirrorless, or video cameras).
  • Understanding of composition, framing, and lighting.
  • Familiarity with audio equipment and basic sound recording.
  • Ability to follow shot lists and direction.
  • Experience with video editing software (preferred).
  • Portfolio or sample reels (preferred).

Benefits:

  • Hands-on experience with professional equipment.
  • Mentorship from senior videographers.
  • Portfolio development through completed projects.
  • Opportunities to learn advanced filming techniques.

3. Photographer

Role Overview:
As a Photographer, you’ll document key moments, create promotional imagery, and contribute to the visual storytelling of our productions.

Responsibilities:

  • Capture high-resolution photos during shoots, events, and rehearsals.
  • Edit and retouch images for use in marketing and social media.
  • Organize and catalog photo assets.
  • Collaborate with directors and social media managers to meet visual needs.
  • Scout locations for optimal photo opportunities.
  • Maintain and care for photography equipment.

Qualifications:

  • Proficiency with cameras (DSLR, mirrorless).
  • Knowledge of photo editing software (e.g., Adobe Lightroom, Photoshop).
  • Strong sense of composition, lighting, and timing.
  • Attention to detail and creativity.
  • Portfolio or sample work (preferred).

Benefits:

  • Real-world experience in event and production photography.
  • Portfolio pieces for future opportunities.
  • Mentorship from professional photographers.
  • Opportunities to showcase work in marketing materials.

4. Editor (Video/Post-Production)

Role Overview:
As a Video Editor, you’ll transform raw footage into polished final products, ensuring each video tells a compelling story.

Responsibilities:

  • Import, organize, and manage video assets.
  • Edit footage for timing, pacing, and narrative flow.
  • Add transitions, effects, graphics, and soundtracks.
  • Color correct and audio balance final cuts.
  • Collaborate with script writers and directors for creative alignment.
  • Export videos in required formats for distribution.

Qualifications:

  • Proficiency with editing software (Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, DaVinci Resolve).
  • Strong sense of storytelling and visual pacing.
  • Attention to detail and technical accuracy.
  • Ability to work under deadlines.
  • Portfolio or sample edits (preferred).

Benefits:

  • Hands-on editing experience with real projects.
  • Feedback and mentorship from experienced editors.
  • Portfolio pieces for future job searches.
  • Exposure to industry-standard workflows.

5. Production Team (Light, Sound, Stage Management)

Role Overview:
As part of the Production Team, you’ll support technical aspects of live and recorded productions, ensuring smooth operations behind the scenes.

Responsibilities:

  • Set up, test, and operate lighting and sound equipment.
  • Assist with stage setup, props, and scenery changes.
  • Monitor and adjust technical elements during rehearsals and performances.
  • Coordinate with cast and crew for timing and cues.
  • Troubleshoot equipment issues as needed.
  • Maintain inventory of production supplies.

Qualifications:

  • Interest in technical theater or live event production.
  • Willingness to learn new equipment and processes.
  • Strong organizational and teamwork skills.
  • Ability to follow instructions and adapt quickly.
  • Prior experience (preferred but not required).

Benefits:

  • Training on professional-grade equipment.
  • Experience in live production environments.
  • Mentorship from technical directors and crew.
  • Opportunities to specialize in lighting, sound, or stage management.

6. Social Media Manager

Role Overview:
As a Social Media Manager, you’ll drive online engagement, promote projects, and build the organization’s digital presence.

Responsibilities:

  • Develop and schedule content across platforms (Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok).
  • Create graphics, captions, and short videos.
  • Monitor analytics and adjust strategies for growth.
  • Engage with followers, respond to comments and messages.
  • Collaborate with photographers and videographers for fresh content.
  • Track trends and incorporate them into campaigns.

Qualifications:

  • Familiarity with major social media platforms and their features.
  • Strong writing and communication skills.
  • Basic graphic design skills (Canva, Photoshop).
  • Creative thinking and adaptability.
  • Experience managing social accounts (preferred).

Benefits:

  • Real-world experience in digital marketing.
  • Portfolio of campaigns and analytics.
  • Mentorship from marketing professionals.
  • Opportunities to experiment with new content formats.

7. Publicity Manager

Role Overview:
As a Publicity Manager, you’ll promote productions and events, build media relationships, and drive public awareness.

Responsibilities:

  • Write press releases, newsletters, and promotional materials.
  • Build and maintain media contact lists.
  • Pitch stories to local and campus media outlets.
  • Organize and manage publicity events (e.g., launch parties, screenings).
  • Track and report on media coverage.
  • Collaborate with social media and production teams for unified messaging.

Qualifications:

  • Strong writing and verbal communication skills.
  • Interest in public relations, marketing, or communications.
  • Organizational skills and attention to detail.
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team.
  • Prior PR experience (preferred but not required).

Benefits:

  • Hands-on PR experience.
  • Portfolio of published materials and media placements.
  • Networking with media professionals.
  • Mentorship from experienced communicators.
